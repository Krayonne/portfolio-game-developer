About Me

Who I am, what I love, and why I make games

Thanadul Suanma | Unity Developer

I'm a 3rd-year Game Design student at SPU who fell down the rabbit hole of Unity and C# and never looked back. I love digging into how games tick — the systems behind the fun, the code that makes a fight feel punchy or a frame rate stay smooth.

Most of my work lives on the programming side: gameplay systems, multiplayer netcode, and squeezing every drop of performance out of a scene. If something's laggy or jank, I want to fix it. That's just who I am.

Outside of pure code, I use tools like Antigravity, Gemini CLI, and Claude to move faster — not to skip the thinking, just to spend more time on the parts that actually matter.

Audio Work

Music and sound I made for my games

Music

Summer Bakery — BGM

The BGM for Summer Bakery. I wanted it to feel warm and a little lazy — like a chill afternoon in a small shop. Trap beat because that's just what came out when I started producing it.

Summer Bakery BGM Trap

Flagellant — Chase Theme

Chase music for a horror game concept. I went full Frenchcore on this one — fast, distorted, relentless. It's supposed to feel like you're running and you know you're not going to make it.

Love's Last Lesson BGM Frenchcore
SFX & Voice

Battle Start

The sound that plays when a wave starts in Abyss of Eden. I layered a bell, a drone, and some heavy strings — then smashed them together until it felt like something big was about to happen.

Abyss of Eden SFX Layering

Death Silent (Ultimate SFX)

The SFX for Death Silent — an ultimate that freezes time. I used ticking clocks, reverse swells, and a whole lot of compression to make it feel like the world just stopped. The panning sweeps left to right to mess with your head a little.

Abyss of Eden SFX Layering

Death Screen Ambient

What plays when you die. I wanted it to feel wrong — like something's off. It's a detuned melody over a dark drone, with distortion cranked up until it feels genuinely uncomfortable to listen to.

Abyss of Eden SFX Layering

Moon Slash (Ultimate SFX)

The SFX for Moon Slash — a big, sweeping sword ultimate. I built it from sword swings and metal impacts, then dumped a ton of dark reverb on top. The tail is distorted and wide so it feels like the attack echoes through the whole arena.

Abyss of Eden SFX Layering

Ultimate Voice Lines

Voice lines for the game's ultimates, processed through VOICEVOX and then heavily treated. Saturated, compressed, and drowning in reverb — I wanted them to sound huge, like a god is talking at you.

Abyss of Eden Voice VOICEVOX
